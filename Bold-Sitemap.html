June — contextual AI entry points

Not just a tab, and not a floating bubble. June’s primary entry is the “Good morning, Carol” greeting on Today — the warmest moment on the page becomes the doorway. A small anchored “Ask June” sits in the header on every screen, and inline action-framed prompts open the same assistant pre-loaded with context, so members never have to explain themselves. Every surface can hand off to a human. (“June” is a placeholder name to A/B test.)

Onboarding
  • Intake: “what does this ask?”
  • Sit-to-stand: “how do I do this safely?”
  • Measurements: “how do I measure my waist?”
  • “What happens at my first visit?”
Active care · daily
  • Side-effect check → explain + escalate
  • Movement: “this hurts my knee — alternative?”
  • Meal photo → “log this for me”
  • Directive: “why 6,000 steps?”
Insights
  • Each trend: “what does this mean?”
  • Explains the member’s own data
  • No jargon, no confidence scores
Care · logistics
  • Appointments: “help me reschedule”
  • Labs: “where’s my nearest Quest?”
  • Refill: “what happens at my review?”
  • Anytime: “get my care team”

Rule: no page is more than 2 taps from Today. Guardrails on every AI surface — never diagnose or prescribe, explain why using the member’s own data, cite reputable sources, fail closed, always offer a human, no emojis, plain language.
